IEntityRecord.cs
¶

TL;DR..

Throughout these documents, an IEntityRecord will be referred to as an entity. You can visualize it as a row in a database table, where each column holds a component.
¶

Similarities and differences

Just like in a regular database, it's not possible to have 2 identical rows. And, in most implementations, it's not possible to attach more than one column/component of the same type to a single entity - though I guess that actually makes it quite dissimilar.

But, since the components are just instances of real classes, they're not limited to being specific datatypes, and instead of simply providing data, they can have behavior too.
